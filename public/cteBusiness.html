JHS Business and Marketing Courses

Introduction to Marketing

In this course, students learn the functions of marketing and the infrastructure of the business world. Students receive an overview of core business concepts, including business environment, ethical practices, business ownership, management, communication and principles of economics. This practical course also teaches students to be able to sell the most important product: themselves.

Marketing 2

Students in this course learn marketing concepts, skills and the underlying foundations of business. Students demonstrate an understanding of sales and marketing concepts, challenge themselves academically, and develop leadership skills and practical business experience through DECA conferences and competition. Students prepare for regional and state DECA competition in class.

Microsoft IT Academy

This course prepares students for the Microsoft Office Suite Certification Exams (MOS) in Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Access (optional). A MOS certification helps validate an individual’s skill in using Microsoft Office and meets the industry demand for the most up to date skills on the latest Microsoft technologies.

Marketing Operations

Student Store students run an authentic retail business. Learning activities include: Cashiering, Food Service, Management, Product Reps, Window Display, Customer Research, Physical Inventory, and Profit & Loss Statements. The Student Store gives marketing students a chance to prove their entrepreneurial skills by producing a profit for their student leadership club DECA.

Digital Design I

Explore the world of digital based communications! Digital Design focuses on the foundations of graphic design and digital communications of the 21st Century. Students explore communication mediums of a digital society using applications such as Adobe Web Premium, Photoshop, and Illustrator, to create projects such as online website development, blogs, social media and wikis.

Career Work Experience

Students who are taking or have taken an occupational course are eligible to register for credit for work experience. Students must be employed in an approved, supervised job. Students must complete a minimum of 180 hours of work for each .50 credit awarded. In addition, students must sign a training agreement and participate in a formal evaluation process to earn credit.
